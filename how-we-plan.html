Planning Timeline

How We Plan

The full annual cycle — from the first grant deadline in early April through the final report in December. Written in month names so it stays accurate year to year.

Festival date

The festival takes place on a Saturday in mid-September through mid-October — whichever Saturday has no Penn State home football game. Confirm the date as soon as the PSU football schedule is released each January, and announce it publicly in late June when applications open.

🌱

Early April — Grant Deadline & Committee Forms

Two things happen at once in early April: the HVAB marketing grant application is due, and the organizing committee comes together for the year. The grant must be submitted regardless of where the committee is — if the full committee isn't assembled yet, the Main Coordinator or Grants Coordinator handles it independently.

  • Submit HVAB marketing grant application — confirm exact deadline each year
  • Required materials: festival description, projected attendance, marketing plan summary, previous year budget or actuals
  • If awarded, note that a post-event report is due in December — calendar it immediately
  • Hold the first committee meeting; assign all roles using the Roles page and Org Chart
  • All new committee members read this full manual before committing to a role
  • Review the previous year's debrief notes and update this manual accordingly
📐

April – May — Internal Planning

The committee is assembled and working, but nothing is public yet. This is the phase for building the internal foundation — the budget, the draft festival structure, the forms, and the outreach materials — so everything is ready to launch publicly in late June.

  • Confirm the festival date (check PSU football schedule); lock it in internally
  • Build or update the festival budget with Finance Coordinator
  • Draft the festival structure: stage layout, vendor zones, info table area, decoration plan
  • Research and begin drafting sponsorship packages
  • Begin artist research and direct outreach — this takes the longest lead time of any area
  • Review and update all Google Forms (vendor, artist, info table, volunteer applications)
  • Website Manager updates the festival site with the new year's information
  • Submit any other grant applications with spring deadlines (Sheetz and others)
  • Prepare borough permit application — gather all required documentation
  • Prepare the public festival date announcement and application launch materials
📣

Late June — Public Launch

This is the moment everything becomes public. The festival date is announced, applications open, sponsor outreach goes out, and the borough permit process formally begins. Coordinate closely so all of these land within the same week — a fragmented launch is harder to promote.

  • Announce the festival date publicly on social media and the website
  • Vendor applications open (Google Form goes live)
  • Information table applications open simultaneously
  • Artist applications open (or direct outreach already underway from May)
  • Volunteer sign-up opens
  • Sponsor outreach packages sent to all prospective sponsors
  • Borough permit application submitted
  • Social media campaign launches — English, Spanish, and Portuguese content
  • Marketing Coordinator coordinates with all area leads to have promo content ready for launch week
🗂

July – Mid-August — Applications Open

Applications are live and the committee monitors and promotes them actively. This is not a passive waiting period — keep promoting, follow up with prospective vendors and sponsors, and stay on top of incoming applications as they arrive.

  • Monitor vendor, info table, and volunteer applications as they come in
  • Follow up with prospective sponsors who haven't responded
  • Continue direct artist outreach; finalize bookings and get contracts signed
  • Social media continues promoting applications with deadlines and reminders
  • Website Manager keeps all forms and pages current
  • Finance Coordinator tracks incoming vendor fees and sponsor payments
  • Borough permit process moves forward — respond to any requests for additional documentation
✅

Mid-August — Applications Close & Confirmations Begin

Applications close around mid-August. The focus shifts from recruiting to confirming — every coordinator locks in their area so there are no open questions going into the final stretch.

  • Vendor applications close; review and send acceptance, waitlist, and rejection notices
  • Verify all required vendor documents (permits, insurance, certifications)
  • Info table applications close; send confirmation packets with table assignments
  • Performance schedule finalized and distributed to all artists with arrival and sound check times
  • Sound, stage, and technical requirements confirmed with sound crew
  • Sponsor agreements signed; benefit delivery underway (logos to Marketing, signage ordered)
  • Print materials designed, approved, and sent to printer — allow at least 2 weeks for turnaround
  • Site map created and shared with vendors, artists, and key contacts
  • Volunteer training sessions scheduled and held
  • Street decoration plan finalized; materials procured
  • Year Honoree recognition details confirmed
📅

2–3 Weeks Out — Final Preparations

  • Final team coordination meeting — all coordinators review festival day responsibilities
  • Confirm every vendor, artist, and info table participant has what they need
  • Distribute site maps and load-in instructions to all participants
  • Social media in full countdown mode — daily posts in the final week
  • Confirm volunteer assignments and send reminder with shift times and check-in location
  • Street decorating crew scheduled and briefed
🎉

Festival Day — Mid-September or October Saturday

All coordinators are present all day. Arrive at your assigned time — vendor load-in starts 4–6 hours before opening. Know your area, know your volunteers, know who to call when something goes wrong.

  • Street decorating final touches (morning of)
  • Vendor load-in and booth inspection
  • Artist arrivals and sound checks
  • Volunteer check-in and station assignments
  • Information table setup
  • Festival opens to the public
  • Coordinators monitor their areas throughout the day
  • Year Honoree recognition ceremony (between sets)
  • Vendor and artist load-out at close
📋

October / November — Post-Festival Debrief

Don't let too much time pass — capture everything while it's fresh. This phase is what makes next year easier and this manual more accurate.

  • Send sponsor thank-you packages within 2 weeks, with festival photos showing their branding
  • Reconcile all finances; finalize budget actuals vs. projected
  • Hold the post-festival debrief — all coordinators attend
  • Document what worked, what was hard, and what to change next year
  • Update this manual based on debrief notes
  • Collect vendor, artist, and volunteer feedback
  • Collect all materials needed for the HVAB grant report now — attendance numbers, photos, budget breakdown, copies of marketing materials produced. December comes fast.
📄

December — HVAB Grant Report Due

If Latin Vibe received the HVAB marketing grant, the post-event report is due in December. Missing this deadline can affect eligibility for future years.

  • Confirm exact deadline with HVAB (noted at time of award)
  • Write the narrative report: what happened, how grant funds were used for marketing, community impact
  • Attach: final attendance numbers, financial accounting, festival photos, copies of marketing materials
  • Submit on time; save a copy in Google Drive — it becomes source material for next April's application
🌱

The Cycle Repeats — Early April

The HVAB grant deadline comes back around. With the December report filed and the debrief notes updated, the application is much faster to write. Confirm the deadline, update the numbers, submit — and hold the first committee meeting for the new year.

Coordination bottleneck to watch: the late June launch

The late June public launch requires every coordinator to have their materials ready at the same time — updated forms, sponsor packages, social media content, the borough permit application. Start preparing these in May so nothing holds up the launch.
