Planning Timeline

How We Plan

A month-by-month guide to what needs to happen and who is responsible for it — from forming the committee through the post-festival debrief.

Festival date: September 12, 2026

All timing below is relative to this date. The earlier a phase starts, the more runway you have when things come up — and things always come up.

🗓

6+ Months Out — Pre-Planning Phase

March 2026 and earlier

This phase sets everything up. If it's rushed or skipped, every later phase is harder. The most time-sensitive item here is grants — many funders have long lead times and strict deadlines.

  • Form the committee and assign all roles at the first meeting
  • Review the previous year's debrief notes and update this manual
  • Build or update the festival budget with Finance Coordinator
  • Begin grant research and submit applications early (Sheetz, HVAB, others)
  • Review and update all Google Forms (vendor, artist, info table applications)
  • Confirm the festival venue and begin permit process with Main Coordinator
  • Begin sponsor outreach — identify targets and send initial packages
  • Confirm the Year Honoree selection process
🚀

3–6 Months Out — Active Planning Phase

April – June 2026

The most intensive coordination period. Multiple processes launch at once and need to stay in sync. The Marketing Coordinator needs content from every other coordinator to promote applications effectively.

  • Vendor applications open (Google Form goes live — 4–5 months out)
  • Artist applications / outreach begins (5–6 months out — via direct contact)
  • Information table applications open at the same time as vendor applications
  • Social media campaigns launch — English, Spanish, and Portuguese content
  • Sponsor commitments are secured; sponsorship agreements signed
  • Volunteer recruitment begins
  • Artist bookings finalized; contracts signed and reviewed by Main Coordinator
  • Review vendor applications; send acceptance/waitlist/rejection notices
  • Website Manager keeps all forms, pages, and schedules current
✅

1–3 Months Out — Pre-Event Phase

July – September 2026

The shift from recruitment to confirmation and logistics. Every coordinator is focused on making sure their area is locked in and all the moving parts work together.

  • All vendor and info table registrations finalized; documents collected
  • Performance schedule confirmed and distributed to artists
  • Sound, stage, and technical needs confirmed
  • Volunteer training sessions held
  • Sponsor benefit delivery underway (logos in materials, signage ordered)
  • Print materials designed, ordered, and distributed (signs, banners, posters)
  • Street decoration plan finalized; materials procured
  • Site map created and shared with all vendors and key contacts
  • Year Honoree recognition ceremony details confirmed
  • Final team coordination meeting — everyone reviews festival day responsibilities
  • Social media ramps up — countdown posts, artist previews, vendor spotlights
🎉

Festival Day — September 12, 2026

All coordinators are present all day. Arrive at your assigned time (vendor load-in starts 4–6 hours before opening). Know your area, know your volunteers, know who to call when something goes wrong.

  • Vendor load-in and booth inspection
  • Artist arrivals and sound checks
  • Volunteer check-in and station assignments
  • Information table setup
  • Street decorating final touches
  • Festival opens to the public
  • Coordinators monitor their areas throughout the day
  • Vendor and artist load-out at close
📋

After the Festival — Post-Event Phase

September 13 onward

This phase is often undervalued but it's what makes next year better. Don't wait — capture insights while they're fresh.

  • Send sponsor thank-you packages within 2 weeks (with festival photos)
  • Reconcile all finances and finalize budget actuals
  • Hold the post-festival debrief meeting — all coordinators attend
  • Document what worked well, what was hard, and what to change
  • Update this manual based on debrief notes
  • File any required grant reports
  • Collect vendor, artist, and volunteer feedback
  • Begin planning for next year if timeline allows

Coordination points to watch

Marketing needs content from every coordinator to post about applications, artists, and vendors. Build in time for the Marketing Coordinator to request — and for other leads to deliver — promo materials, bios, photos, and updates. Delays here cascade quickly.
