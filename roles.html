Who Does What

All committee roles, time commitments, and what each person is responsible for. Read this before the role-assignment meeting so you can make a realistic commitment.

Before you pick a role

Time commitments listed here are for a typical week. In the 6–8 weeks before the festival, expect roughly double the hours. Everyone is expected to be present all day on festival day itself.

Executive Leadership

The person who holds it all together

🏅 Main Festival Coordinator

15–20 hrs/week

This is the top leadership role — usually held by Latin Vibe's Director or a designated senior organizer. This person makes final decisions, handles all money, manages government permits, and is ultimately responsible for the festival succeeding or failing. It is a major commitment and requires someone who can stay calm under pressure, communicate constantly, and solve problems quickly.

  • Access to Latin Vibe's bank account
  • Process all vendor and artist payments
  • Secure government permits
  • Lead major sponsor negotiations
  • Run coordination meetings for all area leads
  • Monitor the overall budget and schedule
  • Make final calls when areas conflict
  • Serve as Latin Vibe's official representative
Core Operations

The engine of the festival

These roles require the most hands-on time and coordination. Both involve managing applications, selecting participants, and being present throughout festival day.

🛍 Vendor Coordinator

8–12 hrs/week

Manages all food and craft vendors — from launching the application to making sure every booth is set up correctly on the day. Requires attention to detail, because food vendors need health permits and insurance documentation that must be verified before they're confirmed.

  • Launch and manage vendor Google Form applications
  • Review applications for cultural fit and quality
  • Verify permits, insurance, and health certificates
  • Assign booth spaces and create site maps
  • Manage load-in, festival hours, and load-out
  • Coordinate vendor payments with Main Coordinator

🎤 Artist & Performances Coordinator

8–12 hrs/week

Builds the performance lineup and manages everything from first contact with artists to their sound check on festival day. This role suits someone with connections in the Latin music scene or strong research skills and comfort with contracts.

  • Research and recruit artists (5–6 months out)
  • Request promo materials and technical riders
  • Negotiate and draft booking contracts
  • Build the performance schedule
  • Coordinate sound and stage technical needs
  • Manage artist arrival, sound checks, and payments
Essential Support

Making it visible and running smoothly

These roles keep the community informed, the website working, and the volunteer force organized. High communication skills are key.

🌐 Website Manager

3–5 hrs/week

Maintains the festival website (hosted on WordPress via ReclaimHosting), updates all application forms in Google Forms, and makes sure information stays current throughout the planning cycle. This role works closely with every other coordinator because they all depend on the website to collect applications.

  • Update Google Forms for vendors, artists, and info tables
  • Publish performance schedules and announcements
  • Keep contact and location info current
  • Troubleshoot form and site issues
  • Support coordinators needing web-based tools

📣 Marketing & Social Media Coordinator

6–10 hrs/week

Runs all social media (Facebook and Instagram), designs and distributes promotional materials, and coordinates with the print materials coordinator on physical signage. Content runs in English, Spanish, and sometimes Portuguese — language skills or access to a translator is a big plus. Campaigns start at least three months before the festival.

  • Build and maintain a social media posting schedule
  • Create content in English, Spanish, and Portuguese
  • Promote vendor, artist, and volunteer applications
  • Coordinate sponsor recognition in posts
  • Handle print material design and production
  • Manage yard signs, banners, and flyers

🙋 Volunteer Coordinator

5–8 hrs/week

Recruits, trains, and schedules all festival volunteers. On festival day, this person is the hub that makes sure every station is staffed and every volunteer knows where to go. Requires strong organizational skills and a friendly, communicative style.

  • Post volunteer opportunities and manage sign-ups
  • Define volunteer roles for each festival area
  • Organize orientation and training sessions
  • Build the festival-day volunteer schedule
  • Serve as the point of contact on festival day
  • Coordinate support requests from other coordinators
Specialist Positions

Focused expertise areas

These roles require specific skills but typically involve fewer weekly hours than core operations roles — except in intensive phases like grant deadlines or pre-event logistics.

💰 Finance Coordinator

4–6 hrs/week

Tracks the budget, monitors expenses, and produces financial summaries. Works closely with the Main Coordinator (who controls the bank account) and all other leads to make sure spending stays on track. Some bookkeeping experience is helpful.

  • Build and maintain the festival budget spreadsheet
  • Track expenses and flag overruns early
  • Collect vendor fee payment records
  • Support financial reporting and post-event reconciliation
  • Coordinate on grant reporting requirements

🏢 Information Tables Coordinator

3–5 hrs/week

Manages community organizations, educational institutions, and nonprofits that want to share resources at the festival. These tables are free or low-cost, serve an educational purpose, and connect attendees to services. Applications open at the same time as vendor applications.

  • Launch and review info table applications
  • Verify alignment with festival mission
  • Assign table locations in coordination with Vendor Coordinator
  • Send confirmation packets to accepted organizations
  • Provide day-of support and check-ins

🤝 Sponsor Relations Coordinator

4–7 hrs/week

Identifies potential sponsors, builds relationships, and makes sure every sponsor gets exactly what was promised to them. This role requires comfort with outreach, negotiation, and follow-through. Strong sponsors are often multi-year relationships, so professionalism matters a lot.

  • Develop tiered sponsorship packages
  • Research and contact prospective sponsors
  • Draft sponsorship agreements
  • Track benefit delivery (logos, signage, social posts)
  • Send thank-you packages and year-ahead save-the-dates

📝 Grants Coordinator

3–6 hrs/week

Researches grant opportunities, manages application deadlines, and tracks reporting requirements. Grant work typically starts 6+ months out because many funders have long lead times. Current prospects include Sheetz and the Happy Valley Adventure Bureau (HVAB) marketing grant.

  • Research grant opportunities relevant to Latin Vibe
  • Track all deadlines in a shared calendar
  • Draft and submit applications
  • Manage compliance and reporting after awards
  • Build a running record of what has and hasn't worked

🌟 Year Honoree Coordinator

2–4 hrs/week

Manages the selection and recognition of the annual Year Honoree — someone who has made a meaningful contribution to the Latin community in the region. The 2026 honoree is Sandra del Pilar. This role involves coordinating the recognition ceremony at the festival.

  • Manage honoree nomination and selection process
  • Communicate with the honoree and their guests
  • Coordinate stage recognition with Artist Coordinator
  • Prepare recognition materials and ceremony details

🎀 Street Decorating Coordinator

2–4 hrs/week (intense pre-festival)

Plans and executes the visual decoration of the festival space in the days before the event. Works closely with the Volunteer Coordinator to recruit the setup crew. Most of the work happens in a short, intensive period right before festival day.

  • Design the decoration plan for the Fraser St. block
  • Source and organize all decoration materials
  • Coordinate with Volunteer Coordinator for setup crew
  • Lead setup in the days before the festival

After the festival — everyone's role

Every coordinator participates in the post-festival debrief. This is where we capture what worked, what didn't, and what to change for next year. The debrief is what keeps this manual useful and accurate — it's not optional.
